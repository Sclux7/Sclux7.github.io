The motif of the "one shot" persists throughout the film, with the motifs presence being especially relevant in the second act of the film in Vietnam as not just the game of Russian Roulette that completely strips the plaeyrs of identity, leaving only the winner and the loser, but exists also in soldiers on both sides chances of survival. Cimino likens this war to the looming chance of that one in six bullet, placing a very real fear directly into the hands of those seeking an opportunity from the war, or an escape from the grim reality that surrounds them, and strips soldiers on both sides of identity, instead making everyone either the winner or the loser, devoid of hope, everything up to chance.
